SIH 2025 — Problem Statement Comparative Table

Four problem statements analyzed for India (Smart India Hackathon 2025). This table includes existing solutions, gaps, end‑users, government initiatives, and elimination status.

Problem Statement Existing Solutions in India Specific Gaps / Limitations Real End‑Users & Needs Government Initiatives / Policies Elimination?
Crowdsourced Civic Issue Reporting & Resolution Several platforms exist: Janaagraha’s I Change My City and MoHUA’s Swachhata app let citizens post civic issues. CRAMAT app on Mobile Seva enables reporting of garbage, potholes, etc. CPGRAMS handles grievances (11.2M cases 2019–24). NGO apps also exist. Forms are complex, many citizens struggle. Apps misused with false “resolved” updates. Low awareness, slow official responses, lack of multilingual/voice support, poor feedback loops. Urban/rural residents need simple, accessible (photo/voice/SMS, local languages) tools with timely updates. Officials need efficient dashboards and tracking. MoHUA’s Swachhata app, CPGRAMS, Smart Cities ICCCs, state/local grievance apps, and Mobile Seva. AI/voice grievance bots being tested. Not Eliminated
Real‑Time Public Transport Tracking (Small Cities) Chalo app (65 cities), Google Transit in metros, apps like Namma BMTC, OneDelhi, EnteKSRTC. State RTCs installing GPS (UPSRTC ~5,000 buses). Pilots in Kolkata with GPS + LED displays. Coverage is patchy in small towns. Reliability issues: buses disappear from maps, wrong ETAs, outdated data. Requires GPS hardware and connectivity. Daily commuters need accurate ETAs, route guidance, crowd/safety info, and SMS/IVR fallbacks. Operators need fleet tracking data. Govt mandates GPS trackers (VLTD, Nirbhaya/FAME). Smart Cities ITS projects, state apps, JNNURM-funded pilots, open data initiatives (e.g., BMTC). Eliminated
Telemedicine Access for Rural Healthcare (Nabha) National eSanjeevani platform, ~300 Punjab HWCs using teleconsults, private telehealth apps (Practo, Mfine), NGO kiosks. Tele‑OPD at PHCs/HWCs. Rural connectivity and literacy issues. Low awareness, language/usability barriers, limited specialty care. Clinics lack equipment. No unified AI/IVR support. Rural residents (elderly, poor) need trusted local‑language phone/video access. Health workers need training and facilitation tools. MoHFW Telemedicine Guidelines (2020), ABDM (2021), eSanjeevani (340M+ consults by 2024), state telemedicine hubs. NITI Aayog supports AI health pilots. Not Eliminated
AI‑Based Farmer Query Support & Advisory Kisan Suvidha app, mKisan SMS portal, Farmer.CHAT (AI), Kisan e‑Mitra Chatbot (govt scheme queries), startups (DeHaat, CropIn). Agritech ML for seeds, weather, remote sensing. Services are generic or one‑way. Trust issues, digital literacy gaps. Reliance on internet/text. Few vernacular voice assistants. Limited personalization and local training data. Small farmers need simple, trusted, localized voice/chat AI (vernacular), offline support, field‑specific advice. Extension officers need AI assist for scale. Government promotes AI in agri (₹990 Cr AI CoEs). Kisan e‑Mitra chatbot, pest surveillance ML, Digital Agri Mission, ATMA, NITI Aayog AI strategy. Not Eliminated
Note: Table based on authoritative reports (2020–2025). Crowdsourced Civic Issue, Telemedicine, and AI‑Advisory remain viable; Real‑Time Transport Tracking is eliminated due to oversaturation and hardware dependency.
