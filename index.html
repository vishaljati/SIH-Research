Crowdsourced Civic Issue Tracker (India) – SIH 2025 Research

Introduction / Summary

Crowdsourcing civic issues empowers citizens to directly report local problems like potholes, garbage dumps, broken streetlights, and water leakages to the authorities. In India, platforms such as the Swachhata App and CPGRAMS have already shown progress in improving citizen–government interaction. The Smart Cities Mission also emphasizes citizen-driven planning and feedback.

However, these systems face challenges: fragmented apps, complex interfaces, limited feedback loops, and exclusion of digitally weaker groups. A unified, AI-powered, multilingual, and accessible platform could bridge these gaps and create a stronger link between citizens and local governments.

Aspect Details
Existing Solutions in India
  • CPGRAMS: Central portal for grievances across government departments (integrated with UMANG, CSCs).
  • Swachhata App: Covers 4,900+ cities for sanitation complaints under Swachh Bharat Mission.
  • Smart City Apps: Examples include Bhopal Plus and Mumbai’s Pothole QuickFix.
  • NGO-led civic tech: e.g., IChangeMyCity (Bengaluru), community-driven reporting with high resolution rate.
Drawbacks of Existing Solutions
  • Complex and lengthy complaint forms; not user-friendly.
  • Minimal feedback or unclear status tracking → low citizen trust.
  • Fragmented platforms (different apps for sanitation, transport, etc.).
  • Accessibility issues for low-literacy or non-smartphone users.
  • Limited multilingual or voice/IVR support.
Government Initiatives / Policies
  • CPGRAMS: Centralized grievance redressal, being upgraded with AI/NLP for better routing.
  • Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban): Includes Swachhata app with SLAs for sanitation complaint resolution.
  • Smart Cities Mission: Citizen-driven planning; use of dashboards & integrated command centers.
  • Digital India / UMANG / MyGov: Grievance modules integrated into national digital platforms.
Real End-Users & Needs
  • Citizens: Need simple, multilingual, real-time reporting with status updates.
  • Marginalized groups: Need IVR/SMS/WhatsApp-based reporting (low digital barriers).
  • Municipal staff/engineers: Require dashboards and auto-escalation workflows.
  • City leaders/administrators: Need analytics and performance monitoring tools.
Specific Gaps / Limitations
  • Data silos: Complaints scattered across different systems.
  • Poor transparency: Citizens often get “closed” status without detailed outcomes.
  • Narrow issue coverage: One app per issue; no universal platform.
  • Access divide: Rural and low-literacy citizens excluded.
  • Language barriers: Many apps still dominated by English interfaces.
  • Weak analytics: Lack of AI-driven insights for proactive governance.

Key Takeaways / Why This Matters for SIH 2025

  • Innovation Space: No existing solution integrates all civic issues with multilingual accessibility, AI-driven insights, and real-time accountability.
  • Hackathon Feasibility: A prototype can be built using MERN stack + AI/ML + open government APIs.
  • Impact: Solves real pain points (trust, accessibility, fragmentation) and aligns with Smart Cities Mission, Swachh Bharat Mission, and Digital India.

Conclusion: The Crowdsourced Civic Issue Tracker is a strong candidate problem statement for SIH 2025.
